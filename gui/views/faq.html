Frequently Asked Questions

1. I have issues with the software. Where can I report it?

If you're having issues with the software, please report the issue here.

2. Do you store any information about peptide sequences in your software?

With regards to data privacy, ACE locally stores peptide sequences on your machine only to proces them, but we do not collect, transmit, or store any peptide sequences or related information externally. Our software is designed to operate within your local machine, ensuring that your sensitive data remains confidential. Furthermore, we open-source our software for transparency. Your data privacy is of utmost importance to us.

3. I generated an ELISpot configuration using ACE but changed some peptide-pool assignments in the actual ELISpot experiment. What can I do to deconvolve immunogenic peptides with these changes?

You can modify the peptide-pool assignments ("assignment" sheet in "ace_elispot_configuration.xlsx") according to your experiment layout and supply the modified file to ACE to deconvolve immunogenic peptides.

4. How can I cite ACE?

If you use ACE, please cite our publication.

5. Why is your software called ACE?

One pleasant summer day in Chapel Hill, NC, the two co-first authors of ACE started learning golf together. That same summer, their lab was trying to generate configurations for high-throughput ELISpot experiments. In golf, the term 'ace' is synonymous with hole-in-one, which aligns with the purpose of ACE to generate ELISpot experiment configurations that can be used to identify immunogenic peptides using as few number of pools as possible.
